Chapters 1-6 Study Guide

Chapter 1 Summary

Jonas, an eleven-year-old boy, lives in a seemingly perfect community where everything is controlled and organized. He feels anxious about the upcoming December Ceremony, where he will receive his life Assignment. The story begins when Jonas sees a mysterious aircraft flying over the community, which is unusual and frightening for everyone. Later, Jonas talks with his family during their nightly ritual of sharing feelings. His father, a Nurturer who cares for babies, mentions a newchild named Gabriel who isn't developing well. Jonas's younger sister Lily shares her anger about a visiting group of Sevens who didn't follow the rules. Jonas describes his feeling as "apprehensive" about the Ceremony, though he's not scaredâ€”just uncertain about his future.

Key Points

  • The community is highly controlled with strict rules
  • Jonas is nervous about the December Ceremony and his Assignment
  • Families share their feelings every evening
  • Jonas's father works as a Nurturer for babies
  • Jonas's feeling is **apprehensive** (nervous but not scared)

Characters

Jonas
The eleven-year-old protagonist who is thoughtful and sensitive. He's waiting to receive his Assignment at the December Ceremony.
Father
Jonas's father, a gentle Nurturer who takes care of newchildren in the community.
Mother
Jonas's mother who works at the Department of Justice.
Lily
Jonas's younger sister who is seven years old. She's energetic and speaks her mind.

Important Vocabulary

Apprehensive
Feeling worried or nervous about something that might happen in the future.
Assignment
The job or role that each person receives at age twelve in the community.
Nurturer
A person whose job is to take care of babies and young children.
Newchild
A baby or very young child in the community.
Precision of Language
The rule of using exactly the right words to express feelings and ideas clearly.

Chapter 2 Summary

Jonas remembers the Ceremony from when Lily became a Seven and received her front-button jacket. He explains how each age group receives something new at the December Ceremonyâ€”younger children get new clothing items, while older ones gain more independence. Jonas recalls a significant event from years ago when a girl named Katharine was "released" from the community after being given the wrong Assignment. His parents help him understand that the Committee of Elders carefully observes everyone to choose the right Assignment. They explain that very rarely, someone can apply for release, but this almost never happens. Jonas's mother reassures him that the Elders never make mistakes and that he shouldn't worry. His father mentions again that he's concerned about Gabriel, the newchild who isn't sleeping well at night.

Key Points

  • Each age group receives specific items at the December Ceremony
  • The **Committee of Elders** observes children carefully before choosing Assignments
  • Release from the community can happen but is very rare
  • The concept of release is introduced but not fully explained

Characters

Committee of Elders
The group of leaders who observe children and decide their Assignments for the future.
Gabriel (Gabe)
A newchild who isn't developing normally and has trouble sleeping at night.
Katharine
A girl from the past who was given the wrong Assignment and later released from the community.

Important Vocabulary

Ceremony
An important formal event where children receive new items or privileges based on their age.
Release
When someone leaves the community permanently (the true meaning is unclear at this point).
Committee of Elders
The group of wise leaders who make important decisions for the community.
Independence
The ability to do things on your own without help from others.
Observe
To watch carefully to learn about someone's abilities and personality.

Chapter 3 Summary

Jonas watches his father bring Gabriel home from the Nurturing Center. He notices something unusual about the newchildâ€”Gabriel has **pale eyes** like Jonas, which is very rare in the community where most people have dark eyes. While playing catch with Asher, Jonas takes an apple home because he notices something strange about it, though he can't explain what. Later, he hears an announcement reminding the community that taking food home is against the rules. Jonas also remembers an incident where he saw something change about the faces of people in the crowd, similar to what happened with the apple. When Jonas sees Larissa, an old woman, at the House of the Old, Fiona tells him that she'll soon be released in a celebration. Jonas is curious about what happens during release ceremonies but doesn't get complete answers.

Key Points

  • Gabriel and Jonas both have unusual **pale eyes**
  • Jonas notices something strange about an apple but can't explain it
  • The community has strict rules about taking food home
  • Jonas is beginning to see things differently from others
  • The mystery of release continues to grow

Characters

Asher
Jonas's best friend who is clumsy with language and very playful.
Fiona
Jonas's friend who has red hair and volunteers at the House of the Old.
Larissa
An elderly woman at the House of the Old who will soon be released.

Important Vocabulary

Pale
Light in color; Gabriel and Jonas have pale (light) eyes instead of dark ones.
Identical
Exactly the same; most people in the community look very similar.
Transgression
Breaking a rule or doing something wrong in the community.
Volunteer Hours
Time that children spend helping in different areas of the community before receiving their Assignment.
House of the Old
The place where elderly people live in the community.

Chapter 4 Summary

Jonas rides his bicycle to volunteer at the House of the Old with his friend Fiona. He enjoys spending time there, especially with the elderly residents. During his volunteer shift, Jonas helps bathe an old woman named Larissa. While bathing her, Larissa tells Jonas about **Roberto's release ceremony** that happened earlier that morning. She describes it as a wonderful celebration where Roberto's whole life was told through speeches and everyone was happy. At the end, Roberto walked through a special door and was released. Larissa doesn't know what's actually behind that door or where people go when they're releasedâ€”no one ever comes back to tell about it. Jonas finds this strange but Larissa seems content and doesn't question it. The conversation shows how the community celebrates the elderly, but the mystery of release becomes even more puzzling.

Key Points

  • Jonas volunteers at the House of the Old and enjoys it
  • Release ceremonies for the elderly are celebrations of their lives
  • No one knows what actually happens during release
  • People in the community don't question what they don't know
  • The community shows respect and care for the elderly

Characters

Larissa
A friendly elderly woman who tells Jonas about Roberto's release ceremony while he bathes her.
Roberto
An old man who was recently released from the community in a celebration ceremony.

Important Vocabulary

Bathing Room
The room where the elderly are washed and cared for by volunteers.
Celebration
A happy ceremony or party to honor someone; release is treated as a celebration.
Telling
The part of a release ceremony where a person's whole life story is shared with everyone.
Nakedness
Being without clothes; considered normal for small children and the Old, but shameful for others.
Serene
Calm, peaceful, and untroubled; how Larissa describes feeling about release.

Chapter 5 Summary

Jonas wakes up from a confusing dream about his friend Fiona. In the dream, he wanted her to get into a bathtub, and he felt strange feelings that he couldn't understand. During the morning ritual of sharing dreams, Jonas tells his family about it. His parents explain that he's experiencing "**Stirrings**"â€”the first feelings of physical attraction that happen during adolescence. His mother gives him a **pill** that he must take every morning to stop these feelings. She explains that everyone in the community takes pills to control the Stirrings once they begin. Jonas feels disappointed because the pleasant feelings from his dream will disappear, but he understands this is how things work in his community. His father also shares that Gabriel will be brought to their home at night to see if extra care can help the newchild develop better.

Key Points

  • Jonas experiences his first **Stirrings** (feelings of attraction)
  • The community controls feelings through **daily pills**
  • Everyone must take pills once Stirrings begin
  • Jonas feels sad about losing the pleasant feelings
  • Gabriel will come to Jonas's home at night for extra care

Characters

Jonas
Experiencing his first Stirrings and beginning to notice how much the community controls.
Mother
Explains about Stirrings and gives Jonas his first pill to control feelings.

Important Vocabulary

Stirrings
The first feelings of physical attraction and desire that happen during adolescence.
Treatment
The pills that people take to control Stirrings and other unwanted feelings.
Ritual
A regular ceremony or practice; families share dreams and feelings every morning and evening.
Adolescence
The time when young people develop from children into adults.
Regulated
Controlled by rules or medications; the community regulates feelings and behavior.

Key Points

  • The December Ceremony is the community's biggest annual event
  • Each age group receives something that shows growing maturity
  • Asher becomes **Assistant Director of Recreation**
  • **Jonas is mysteriously skipped** during the Assignment ceremony
  • Jonas feels humiliated and scared about being left out
